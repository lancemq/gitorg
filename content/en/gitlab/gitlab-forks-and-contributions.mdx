# GitLab Forks and Contribution Flow

## When this workflow shows up

Use this path when contributors do not have direct write access to the target project, or when teams want external contribution separated from internal branches.

In GitLab, that usually also means thinking about:

- group and project permission boundaries
- merge-request targets
- CI and variable behavior in forked contexts

## A steadier contribution rhythm

1. fork the project
2. clone your fork
3. add the upstream remote
4. sync the main line
5. create a working branch
6. push to your fork
7. open the merge request back upstream

## Why permissions and CI matter here

Fork-based contribution on GitLab often becomes tricky because teams overlook:

- who can trigger which pipelines
- whether variables or secrets are available
- which project the merge request actually targets

That is why external contribution flows need stronger operational caution than internal team branching.

## Common mistakes

- never syncing upstream
- confusing the contributor fork with the target project
- trusting CI results without understanding fork-related runner and variable limits
